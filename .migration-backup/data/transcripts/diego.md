# User Research: Diego Velasquez, Tour Manager

**Interviewer:** Anil (Greenroom PM)
**Date:** Late January 2026, 20 minutes, phone call between tour stops
**Diego is currently touring with Pale Lake. He's TM'd ~200 shows across small and mid-cap venues over five years.**

---

**Anil:** Thanks for jumping on. I know you're between shows.

**Diego:** Yeah, we've got Atlanta tonight and then a long drive to Birmingham. So we have like 18 minutes before I gotta be up.

**Anil:** I'll be fast. I want to understand the settlement experience from your side. From the tour manager's seat, what does a good settlement feel like? What does a bad one feel like?

**Diego:** Okay. Good one — the booker has all the numbers ready when I walk into the office. They already know what their final settlement is. We sit down, they walk me through it line by line, I check the math, I sign it, we move on. Should take 20 minutes max. Good ones are at venues where the booker is on top of it.

Bad one — the booker doesn't have expense numbers yet, they're like "give me a sec to pull this up." So we sit there while they're texting the production manager about hospitality. Then they tell me the math and I check it on my phone calculator and the numbers don't match and now we're debating which expense is supposed to come off of where. By the time we're done it's 2:30am, I'm exhausted, the artist is annoyed, and I'm not sure I trust the number we landed on.

**Anil:** What goes wrong most often?

**Diego:** Three things. One: ambiguous deal terms. The deal email from the agent has some sentence that means different things to me and the venue, and we have to figure out which read we're going with at the table. That should never happen at the table — that should have been resolved when the deal was negotiated.

Two: expenses showing up that I didn't know about. Hospitality went over budget, marketing recoup wasn't called out, some production charge that wasn't in the original advance. I get that stuff happens, but I don't want to find out at 2am.

Three: the booker's math is opaque. They give me a number and I have no idea how they got there. If I have to ask "where did the $14,427 come from" and they have to explain it for ten minutes from memory, I'm not signing that. I want to see the math.

**Anil:** What do you bring with you to a settlement?

**Diego:** A laptop, the deal memo from our agent, a calculator app, and a number I expect to land somewhere near. The artist's manager and I have already done back-of-envelope math earlier in the week. I have an expected range. If the venue's number is in the range, we're fine. If it's outside, that's where the conversation gets long.

**Anil:** When you don't trust a venue's settlement, what happens next?

**Diego:** I sign it, because the artist needs to load out and we're driving overnight. Then in the morning I tell the agent what happened. The agent decides whether to push back. If they do, the venue is in for a difficult email thread the next week, and that venue gets put on a list — not a blacklist exactly, but a list of venues we don't route through unless we have to.

**Anil:** Have you ever had a settlement go really well at The Crescent?

**Diego:** Yeah, mostly they go well. Mariana is one of the good ones. She's on top of her numbers, she shows her work, she's honest about what she's absorbing versus passing through. I trust her math because she shows me. The 2am part is still the 2am part — I'm tired, she's tired — but the *conversation* is fine.

The thing I don't get is why she's doing it in a Google Sheet. Like, she has all this software, why is she using a spreadsheet? But I get it — the spreadsheet does what she needs.

**Anil:** What would make this experience materially better for you?

**Diego:** Being able to look at the math before I sit down. Like, on the drive between load-out and the back office, I'd love to be able to pull up the venue's settlement on my phone and look at it. Right now everything happens in real time across the table from each other. If I could pre-review and we could just discuss the line items I'm confused about, the conversation would be 10 minutes instead of 45.

Honestly? I'd also love to not have it be at 2am. But that's not really a software problem.

**Anil:** Last thing — what's the worst settlement experience you've ever had?

**Diego:** [laughs] One venue in Texas, three years ago, the booker sat down with me, looked at the numbers for the first time, and said "I'm not really sure how to calculate this deal." She didn't know what an 80/20 vs deal was. She was new. And I'm sitting there trying to teach her how settlement math works while also being on the receiving end of the math. That was bad. Took two hours.

The Crescent is not that. Mariana's good. The system around her is just kind of a mess.

**Anil:** Useful. Thanks Diego.

**Diego:** Yeah, no worries. Build something good.
