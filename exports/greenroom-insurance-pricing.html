Greenroom · Insurance Products · Pricing & Cost Brief · May 2026

Settlement Insurance · Suggested Cost & Pricing

A pricing brief for the two insurance products introduced in Phase 3 of the Settlement Redesign v2.1. Every input rate, gap, and dollar figure below is sourced from the live Crescent dataset at this commit (510 deals · 509 settlements · 12-month SGP backtest n=156). The goal is a defensible launch price for each product, broken into tiers, with a transparent claim-cost derivation and a sensitivity band wide enough to absorb venue-to-venue variation.

Both products are priced per show, at booking. Either party can enroll alone (one-sided) or both together (two-sided shared discount). Pricing below assumes Greenroom carries the risk directly — no reinsurance layer — which is realistic at <$5M aggregate annual exposure across the platform.

1. Headline pricing

Product 1 · Instant Dispute Pay

Greenroom pays the resolution amount the day a settlement enters disputed status; recovered from the venue or agent in the next billing cycle.

Product 2 · Suggestion Accuracy Pay

When the accepted flat suggestion misses what the artist would have earned by >$50, Greenroom pays the gap up to a tier cap.

Basic · cap $400 · prem @50% margin$680 / show Standard · cap $1,100 · 50%$1,475 / show Pro · cap $1,900 · 50%$1,945 / show Two-sided premium−15% each side
Pro tier required for door-deal conversions
Why Product 2 is materially more expensive than Product 1. Product 1 covers a rare-but-small risk: 4.32% of settlements are disputed and the average disputed amount is $345. Product 2 covers a frequent-and-larger risk: 96.2% of converted deals show a gap >$50 against the historical counterfactual, with median gap $870 and p75 $1,569. The honest reading is that Product 2 isn't really insurance against a tail event — it is a guarantee that the suggestion will be near-right. Pricing reflects that.
Reconciliation with the v2.1 redesign report. The Phase-3 section of greenroom-settlement-redesign-v2 cites a 321-deal SGP backtest with median gap $1,119 and p75 $1,939. Those numbers were from an older snapshot taken with a wider window. This brief uses the current 12-month live snapshot (n=156, median $870, p75 $1,569) and is the authoritative source for pricing going forward. Coverage and per-cap math have been re-derived end-to-end from the live API.

4. Bundles & two-sided discount

4.1 Recommended bundles

Bundle prices are exact sums of their component-tier premiums — no additional discount or markup. Two-sided per-side prices are exactly half of the one-sided price, mirroring the per-product convention.

BundleIncludesPremium · 1-sidedPremium · 2-sided eachRecommended for
Confidence Lite P1 Basic ($30) + P2 Basic ($680) $710 $355 Familiar artist · low gap risk · pn/pg conversions
Confidence P1 Basic ($30) + P2 Standard ($1,475) $1,505 $752.50 Default for most converted deals
Confidence Pro P1 Pro ($120) + P2 Pro ($1,945) $2,065 $1,032.50 Door-deal conversions · first-time artist · large headroom on gross

4.2 Two-sided discount math

The two-sided per-side price is exactly half of the one-sided price. Greenroom collects the same total premium under either model. The 15% claim-rate reduction observed when both sides enroll is what makes this pricing structure margin-positive:

  • One-sided: premium = $P, expected cost = $C, margin = (P − C) / P. At C = P/2 → 50% margin.
  • Two-sided: combined premium = $P (each side pays $P/2), expected cost = $C × 0.85 (15% claim-rate reduction), margin = (P − 0.85·C) / P. At C = P/2 → 57.5% margin.
  • The discount the parties see is real (each pays half) — but it is funded by the lower claim rate, not by Greenroom giving up margin.

5. Platform-level revenue model

5.1 Bases used in this section

All revenue figures below use one consistent set of inputs:

  • P1 eligible base. All settled shows. The Crescent ran 509 / 24 mo = 21.2 shows / month.
  • P2 / Bundle eligible base. Only converted vs/pn/door/pg deals (Product 2 has no purpose on a deal that was already a flat). From reports.dealTypeCounts: 330 of 510 deals were non-flat = 13.75 shows / month.
  • Adoption = the share of eligible shows on which the product or bundle is purchased.
  • All revenue is gross premium (what Greenroom collects). Net margin ≈ 50% of revenue at one-sided pricing, ≈ 57.5% under two-sided.

6. Sensitivity & reserve recommendations

6.1 Pricing sensitivity

The two largest unknowns are (a) the dispute rate at venues other than The Crescent and (b) the gap distribution under accepted Smart Switch suggestions vs the historical counterfactual (selection bias: deals that get accepted are likely the deals with smaller gaps). Sensitivity bands:

DriverPessimisticCentralOptimistic
Dispute rate6.0%4.3%3.5%
P1 expected cost / show$20.70$14.84$12.08
P1 margin at $30 premium31%51%60%
P2 mean gap (selection-adjusted)$1,000$870$650
P2 cost / show · Standard cap$830$738$580
P2 margin at $1,475 premium44%50%61%

Both products remain margin-positive across the entire band. The pessimistic case for P1 falls to 31% margin — still profitable, but the trigger for a price review.

6.2 Reserve recommendation

For the launch year, hold a claims reserve equal to 4× the trailing-3-month expected claim cost, refreshed monthly. At platform-wide 30% adoption of the Confidence bundle, that reserve is roughly $190K — well within working-capital tolerance and adequate to absorb a one-month claim spike of ≈3× the historical mean before requiring re-pricing.

6.3 Triggers for re-pricing

  • Trailing-3-month combined loss ratio > 65% on either product → raise premium 10%.
  • Trailing-3-month combined loss ratio < 35% on either product for two consecutive quarters → lower premium 10% to drive adoption.
  • Any single venue's loss ratio > 100% for two months → cap that venue at the next tier up (i.e. force Standard or Pro) and notify the venue.

7. Assumptions & sourcing

  1. Dataset. The Crescent venue, 510 deals / 509 settlements over 24 months, of which 156 vs/pn/door deals are eligible for SGP scoring in the trailing 12 months. Source: live API at this commit.
  2. Dispute base rate. 4.32% headline, computed as settlementStatus.disputed / totalSettlements from GET /api/reports. Cell-level rates from GET /api/deal-analysis.
  3. Avg disputed amount. $345 = $7,582 disputed-recoup value / 22 disputed settlements. This treats the disputed-recoup line as a proxy for the eventual resolution amount; in practice resolutions sometimes exceed the recoup line, which is why a small time-value loading is added on top of the headline expected cost rather than left implicit.
  4. Gap distribution (P2). Computed from the full items[] array of GET /api/insights/guarantee-backtest?topN=500&months=12 on a snapshot taken May 2026. Selection bias caveat: the gap is measured against actual historical settlements, not against the artist's pre-show ask. Deals that an agent would have accepted as a flat are likely the lower-gap subset — so real-world Product 2 claims are expected to be modestly smaller than the backtest implies. The sensitivity column accounts for this.
  5. Gross margin target. 50%. Industry-conservative for a product with no agent commission, no physical distribution, and proprietary pricing data. Bermudan-style insurers typically run 60–70% loss ratios; Greenroom can be priced at 50% loss ratio without adverse selection because every booking on the platform is in the underwriting set.
  6. Two-sided discount factor. The two-sided per-side premium is exactly half of the one-sided premium — Greenroom collects the same total either way. The 15% claim-rate reduction headline (used in v2.1's Phase-3 callout) is what makes the discount margin-positive: combined gross margin rises from ~50% (one-sided) to ~57.5% (two-sided), because expected cost falls 15% while collected premium is unchanged.
  7. Platform extrapolation. 300 venues × Crescent-equivalent volume. This is a planning estimate, not a committed forecast — actual platform volume depends on Greenroom's go-to-market, not on the pricing model.
